Things you need to know

    During the job interview, you will be asked to ask the employer a few questions. This is the perfect opportunity for you to learn more about the position of the job you are applying for; as well as know more about the actual operations of the business. However, you will easily lose points when asking about something that you have not really thoroughly investigated before. Therefore, before the interview, you need to read the basic information about the company on the website first, learn the mission, mission of the organization and carefully read the specific description of the job being applied. If you want to be better prepared, you can read the recent news of the company to get the situation and exchange more with employers during the interview process.
    Punctuality is important for any employer. If you are late for the interview, the employer will evaluate you not to appreciate the interview. How can they believe you will work on time in the following days? To avoid this error, you should see the road map to the interview location at least one day in advance. You can also use the map and find your way to make sure you don't get lost. Ideally, before the interview you should take the time to get to the interview site first to see how long it will take to get there. Calibrate backup time, ensure adequate fuel to avoid running out of gas in the middle of the road!
    When you and many people always assume one thing, when you go to the interview, you have to dress politely, appropriately, There are still people interviewing with wrinkled costumes, torn jeans or wet hair. To make a good impression, before the interview, your clothes intended to be worn should be kept clean, flat, Be polite and fit with your position. Avoid using perfume too harsh or inappropriate - that will be your minus point.
    Employers are always eager to work with positive people and teammates who work effectively. Even if you are asked to answer the reason why you left the nearest job, do not add any negative comments in your answer. This will make you worse. During the interview process, avoid this complaint about your former boss, co-worker or previous work environment too much. Employers will definitely not expect to hear you talk about them like that to another employer when you are not working there anymore. Reply stereotyped Nothing is wrong when preparing for a job interview. However, you should not prepare too much for common questions because employers always want you to express your true self, not your reading an article and finding the answer. best for the question: "What is your weakness?" During the interview, use your own stories, related to the work you do to make your answers more colorful.
